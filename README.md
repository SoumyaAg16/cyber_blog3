#Cybersecurity's Paper Tigers

Malware is a type of software program, also known as "malicious software." Malicious software can access your private files and transfer them to another device. Through it, hackers can steal your information, photos, videos, and details related to your bank accounts.

#Types of Malware

#Virus – A virus poses the greatest threat to a local computer.

#Worms – A key characteristic of worms is their ability to create multiple copies of themselves; essentially, they spread rapidly.

#Trojan Horse – Once installed on a computer, it gains control over the system.

#Ransomware – Once installed, it locks the computer by encrypting all the data.

#Spyware – Its function is to monitor activities and data and transmit them to a third party.

#Adware – Its purpose is not to damage data; instead, once installed, it causes advertisements to appear on your home screen without you even opening an app.

Ransomware involves a program entering a computer without your permission and locking all the data stored on it. A threatening message is then displayed, stating that a ransom must be paid to recover these files.

I would like to view this attack from two perspectives. First, cybercriminals are extremely cunning and remain twenty steps ahead of security officials. Second, cybercriminals utilize the dark net to avoid detection.

The ransomware attack of 2017 affected 99 countries. It is considered one of the largest attacks in human history. This attack caused global alarm and brought operations at numerous institutions to a complete standstill.

The ransomware attack in July 2024 severely disrupted the operations of at least 150–200 cooperative banks and Regional Rural Banks (RRBs). The ransomware attack targeted C-Edge Technologies Limited.

#Ransomware Variants

#Crypto Locker – Credited with ushering in the modern era of ransomware in 2013.

#WannaCry - The ransomware attack of 2017 affected 99 countries.

#Ryuk – Popularized "big-game" ransomware attacks targeting high-value entities.

#DarkSide – Responsible for the Colonial Pipeline attack in 2021.

#Locky – Uses macros in email attachments to infect devices.

#Protection against Malware attacks

#Data backup is essential – Despite various security measures, the threat of Malware continues to rise;  therefore, always maintain a backup of your data.

#Keep software updated – Whether using a new or old phone or PC, regular software updates are crucial.

#Use a network firewall – This prevents unauthorized users from accessing your website, mail server, and other information. Additionally, do not forget to secure your wireless network.

#Malware has emerged as one of the most disruptive and financially damaging cyber threats of the modern era. As Malware continues to evolve, individuals, businesses, and governments must remain vigilant and invest in both technology and cyber resilience. The growing sophistication of cybercriminals, combined with their ability to operate anonymously through the dark web, makes combating these attacks increasingly challenging.
